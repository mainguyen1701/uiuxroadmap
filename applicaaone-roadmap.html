Critical findings
CriticalNo value proposition above the fold
Every page shares the same H1: "Search. Compare. Apply." — a tagline that assumes visitors already know what ApplicaaOne is. First-time visitors from search or social have no context. UCAS and Coursera both lead with a clear headline explaining who the platform is for and what it does.

Homepage comparison: UCAS says "Find your next step in higher education" — ApplicaaOne says a three-word tagline.
Distinct H1 per page. Homepage: "Find your perfect sixth form or college — and apply in minutes." Courses: "Search 45,000+ Courses Across the UK." Events: "Open Days & Events Near You."
CriticalTrust signals are backfiring — 105 reviews for 45,495 courses
The stats row prominently shows: 45,495 Courses · 158 Subjects · 105 Reviews · 95% Satisfaction. A 95% satisfaction rate from 105 reviews is not credible to a modern internet user — that is 0.23% review coverage. The two homepage reviews read "BEST SCHOOL" and "Is good" with placeholder avatars. These undermine trust more than having no reviews at all. Stats are also completely hidden on mobile (hidden md:grid).
Replace raw review count with credible trust signals: "Trusted by 500+ UK schools", "Join 12,000 students who found their place this year". Minimum 50-word reviews, verified by event attendance. Show 2 condensed stats on mobile.
CriticalNavigation fails first-time visitors — no product nav exists
The full-width nav contains only: Logo · Log In · Sign Up. No product links — Courses, Events, Careers, For Schools are absent. Users who land on any page other than the homepage have no way to navigate without using the browser back button. The "For Schools" B2B audience is entirely ignored in navigation.
Add primary nav: Find Schools · Browse Courses · Open Days · Careers · For Schools. Make sticky on scroll. Mobile: hamburger drawer + bottom tab bar.
CriticalDesign token chaos — 7 ways to reference blue
Colour audit found: text-primary-5 (26 uses), text-brand-blue (5 uses), text-blue-5 (3 uses), text-blue-600 (3 uses), bg-[#2D8FC5] (1), bg-[#1E2F72] (1), bg-blue-500 (1). Changing the brand colour requires touching 7 different class types. Same fragmentation exists in typography — 77 instances of text-sm alongside hardcoded text-[24px] and text-[32px].
Consolidate to 3 brand colour tokens: $color-brand-primary (#263C91), $color-brand-secondary (#2D8FC5), $color-brand-dark (#1E2F72). Define 7-level typographic scale and enforce via DS.
HighHomepage has no journey — 2 reviews and 1 card below the fold
Content below the hero: stats row (desktop only) · 2 low-quality reviews · 1 "Career Exploration" card. Airbnb, Coursera, and UCAS all follow a clear pattern: Hero → How it works → Categories → Featured → Proof → CTA. ApplicaaOne ends at the hero for most users.
Full homepage journey: 1) Hero + search, 2) How it works (3 steps), 3) Browse by school type/subject, 4) Featured open days this week, 5) Verified student outcomes, 6) Credible trust signals, 7) CTA banner.
HighCTA hierarchy is broken — Log In and Sign Up look identical
Both nav buttons are solid filled buttons of similar size and weight. Neither reads as primary vs secondary. Across the site, "OK I got it", "Back to Tracking", "Send More Interest", and "Sign Up" all render at similar visual weight — users cannot quickly identify the most important action on any screen.
Primary CTA: Sign Up — filled, brand colour. Secondary: Log In — text-only or ghost. Apply consistent CTA hierarchy across all pages and modals.
HighRegistration page is blank on server render
The registration page renders with empty headings — the entire form depends on JavaScript. If JS is slow or fails, users see a blank page. Critical conversion pages must have server-rendered content. Currently unindexable by search engines.
Server-render the registration type selection (Student / School / Parent) as static HTML. Progressively enhance with JS.
MediumSame boilerplate content on every page
Every page ends with identical sections: "Recent Reviews From Community" (same 2 reviews) and "More Ways We Can Help You" (same 1 Career Exploration card). The Career Exploration card is irrelevant on a course search page and shows the product was not fully content-designed per page.
Make review section dynamic and page-contextual. Show relevant "More Ways" cards per page type (on /courses show "See Open Days" and "Apply Now").
MediumSearch bar collapses poorly on mobile
The pill search bar with 4+ fields was designed for 1200px+. On a 375px phone, 64px horizontal padding leaves only 247px for 4 fields plus a search button. Stats are entirely hidden on mobile (hidden md:grid). Most student visitors are on their phones.
Simplified mobile search: single "Where are you looking?" field that opens a full-screen filter modal on tap — the pattern used by Airbnb, Booking.com, and Google Flights.
✓ Best page on site — Careers (/careers_hubs)
The careers page is the strongest on the site: distinct purpose, clear industry categories, growth data, career pathways with subject connections, and a functional search. Improvements: add salary ranges, link careers back to relevant courses and schools, add required A-Levels for each pathway, personalise based on subjects the student is studying.

Phase 0 — Critical Fixes

Six independently shippable fixes that address the most damaging issues identified in the audit — the ones actively costing conversions and undermining trust right now. No redesign required. Each fix can ship without waiting for anything else.

🏷️
Distinct H1 per page
Every page currently uses "Search. Compare. Apply." — a three-word tagline that tells first-time visitors nothing about what they're looking at. Google penalises duplicate H1s. Screen reader users can't understand page context. Fix: unique, descriptive H1 on every page.
Immediate SEO benefit. Users oriented on every page. Screen reader users understand context.
⭐
Replace weak trust signals
105 reviews for 45,495 courses is 0.23% coverage. The two visible homepage reviews — "BEST SCHOOL" and "Is good" — actively damage credibility. The stats row is hidden on mobile entirely. Replace with credible, verifiable signals: school count, verified student outcomes, partner organisations.
Trust signals that hold up to scrutiny. Stats visible on mobile. Credibility where it matters most — above the fold.
↑ Parent confidence↑ Registration conversion
🧭
Product navigation
The nav bar has no links to the product — only Logo, Log In, Sign Up. Users who land on any page other than the homepage have no way to navigate. The B2B "For Schools" audience is entirely invisible. Fix: full product nav, sticky on scroll, mobile drawer and bottom tab bar.
Users can navigate from any page. Schools can find the B2B proposition. Bounce rate from direct and search traffic reduced.
🎨
Design token cleanup
7 different references to blue. Typography mixes Tailwind scale classes with hardcoded pixel values. Changing the brand colour requires touching 7 different class types. Consolidate to 3 colour tokens and a defined typographic scale — the foundation for all future design work.
Consistent visual appearance across the site. Future design changes take hours, not weeks. No more unintentional colour drift.
👆
CTA hierarchy fix
Sign Up and Log In look identical. Across all pages, "OK I got it", "Back to Tracking", "Send More Interest", and "Sign Up" render at similar visual weight. Users scan for the primary action — if they can't find it instantly, they don't complete it. Fix: clear primary vs secondary button distinction everywhere.
Sign-up CTA stands out clearly. Users complete intended actions faster. Fewer accidental "Log In" clicks when trying to sign up.
↑ Sign-up rate↓ Navigation errors
📋
Server-render registration
The registration page is blank without JavaScript. A student on a slow school Wi-Fi network sees an empty page on the most critical conversion screen on the site. The page is also unindexable by search engines. Fix: server-rendered HTML for the Student/School/Parent type selection, progressively enhanced with JS.
Registration works on slow connections. Page is indexable. No blank-screen abandonment at the most important step.

Phase 1 — Discovery and Conversion

Improve the core user journeys that drive students and parents to engage, register, and apply. Homepage, course search, events, and registration — each redesigned to remove friction and guide users toward a clear next step.

🏠
Homepage — full content journey
The homepage currently ends after the hero. Airbnb, Coursera, and UCAS all follow a deliberate journey from hero to conversion. ApplicaaOne needs the same: a hero that explains who this is for, a How It Works section, categories to browse, featured open days this week, verified outcomes, credible trust signals, and a final CTA banner.
Students and parents have a reason to scroll and a clear next step at every point. First-time visitors understand the platform in under 10 seconds.
↑ Time on page↑ Search and browse engagement↑ Account creation
🔍
Mobile search — simplified
The 4-field pill search bar fits on a 1200px desktop. On a 375px phone, it doesn't work. Most students are on their phones. Replace with a single field ("Where are you looking?") that opens a full-screen filter modal on tap — the same pattern Airbnb, Booking.com, and Google Flights use.
Search works on every device. Mobile students can filter by location, type, and subject without a broken layout.
📚
Course discovery — level filters and save
No way to filter by qualification level (A-Level, BTEC, T-Level, GCSE). No way to save or bookmark without an account — this adds friction before any value has been delivered. Students should be able to build a shortlist before registering.
Filter by level, subject, location, entry requirements. Save to shortlist without an account (cookie-based). Account creation prompted naturally when saving the second course.
📅
Events page redesign
Events currently have no map view (essential for location-based choices), no calendar view (hard to spot clashes), no RSVP without an account (friction before value), and no quick link to the school profile from an event card.
List/Map/Calendar toggle. RSVP without account — email capture, convert later. Quick profile link on every event card. Add to calendar button.
✍️
Registration — 3 fields only
Registration asks for too much upfront. Students who have just found a course they're excited about are stopped by a lengthy form. The cognitive load at this moment should be minimal — name, email, year group is all that's needed to create an account.
3 fields: name, email, year group. All other data collected at the application stage. Google/Microsoft SSO available. Completion rate significantly improved.
↑ Registration completion↓ Drop-off
📄
Page-contextual content
Every page ends with the identical "Recent Reviews" section (2 reviews) and "Career Exploration" card regardless of context. A Career Exploration card at the bottom of a course search page is irrelevant. Content below the fold should reinforce what the user was doing, not redirect them.
Reviews section shows relevant school/subject reviews per page. "More Ways" section suggests contextually logical next steps. Each page feels purposeful to the bottom.

Phase 2 — Product Redesigns

Full redesigns of the core product screens: school profiles, course detail, verified reviews, student dashboard, and school-facing analytics. These are the screens where users make decisions — they need to be significantly better than they are today.

🏫
School profile pages
All profiles look identical — a name and a list. Parents and students choosing a school for two years of post-16 education need to understand what makes each school different. Photo hero, key stats (Ofsted rating, school size, catchment), filterable course list, upcoming open days, verified reviews, persistent Apply/Book Visit CTA.
Differentiated school profiles. Parents make informed comparisons. Open day bookings increase from profile pages.
↑ Open day bookings↑ Application conversion
📖
Course detail pages
Course cards link to minimal pages. Students choosing between A-level options need entry requirements, qualification details, teacher information (where available), and related courses at the same school. Currently none of this is available.
Full course detail: entry requirements, qualification type and level, school context, related courses, direct apply CTA. Students make confident choices.
⭐
Verified review system
Reviews gated by confirmed open day attendance. Minimum 50 words. Rating across defined dimensions (teaching quality, facilities, pastoral support, distance from home). Reviewer shown as year group only (not name) for privacy. Trust score surfaced prominently on school profiles and in search results.
Reviews that hold up to scrutiny. Parents trust them because they know how they were collected. Target: 4,000+ verified reviews in the first academic year.
↑ Parent trust↑ Time on school profiles
📊
Student dashboard
After registering, students have no reason to return. No shortlist management, no application tracking, no offer visibility. The dashboard needs to become the place students return to throughout the admissions cycle — saved schools, course shortlist, active applications with status, upcoming open days, offer management.
Students return to the platform throughout the admissions cycle. High return rate. Application completion improves because students have a single place to track everything.
💼
Careers page enhancements
The strongest page on the site but missing key information students want: salary ranges, required A-Level or vocational qualifications for each career, direct links to relevant courses and schools, and personalisation based on subjects the student is currently studying.
Salary ranges added. Each career pathway links directly to relevant courses. Personalised based on saved subjects. Careers → Courses → Apply becomes a coherent journey.
🎓
School-facing dashboard
Schools have no visibility of how their ApplicaaOne profile performs. How many views? How many interest clicks? How many RSVP-to-application conversions? Without this data, schools cannot improve their profiles and have no reason to invest in them.
School admin view: profile views, interest clicks, event RSVP rates, application conversion. Actionable improvement tips. Schools actively invest in their profiles.
↑ School engagement↑ Profile quality

Phase 3 — Scale and Personalisation

Platform-wide quality pass and personalisation. Accessibility compliance, full mobile device testing, personalised recommendations based on student profile, and design system governance to ensure the improvements from Phases 0–2 hold up as the team grows.

♿
WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility audit
Audit identified: star ratings use icon classes only with no text equivalent, disabled "Search within" field has tooltip-only help, form error announcements missing for screen readers. Full audit across all pages and student flows. All critical findings fixed before exam results day traffic.
Platform accessible to students with visual, motor, and cognitive impairments. Accessibility statement published.
📱
Mobile — full product pass
Phases 0–2 address the highest-impact mobile issues (search, navigation, registration). Phase 3 is a comprehensive pass across every screen. Tested on iPhone, Android, and iPad. Tables replaced with card layouts. Tap targets meet minimum size. No horizontal overflow on any screen.
Complete mobile experience. Students can use every feature of the platform on their phone without workarounds.
✨
Personalised recommendations
Every student currently sees identical content regardless of their subjects, location, year group, or past behaviour. No personalisation means no reason to return between sessions. Build a recommendation layer: "Because you're studying English and History — here are A-level courses nearby."
Personalised homepage and dashboard per student. "Because you saved X" content sections. Students return during the admissions cycle rather than visiting once and leaving.
↑ Return visits↑ Application completion
📖
DS governance and contribution guide
7 blue token variants is the direct result of no design contribution process. Without governance, the same fragmentation will return in Phase 0–2 improvements. Publish naming conventions, documentation requirements, and PR criteria. Automated linter blocks hardcoded colour and font values from being merged.
Token system stays clean. New contributors follow a clear process. Visual consistency maintained as the team grows.
