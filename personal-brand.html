Brand identity
Who Emma is — in one sentence
Not a credentials story. Not an origin story. An ongoing process.
A designer who examines her own thinking as rigorously as she examines a product — and shares what she finds.
You don't just grow — you notice yourself growing. You archive roadmaps when they no longer serve. You watch session recordings when the data doesn't make sense. You ask why the plan was wrong, not just that it was wrong. That quality of self-reflection is rare, and it's what makes everything else you do more credible.

This is not a highlight reel. It's the actual process of changing your mind, updating your assumptions, and moving forward with more clarity than before.

Content pillars
3 story themes
Every post belongs to one of these. Not topics — recurring chapters in the same ongoing story.
Theme 1
When I was wrong
Specific moments of changed thinking. Not failures — shifts. The plan that got archived. The screen that kept failing until you looked upstream. The workflow you abandoned for a better one.
"What changed, and what it taught me."
Theme 2
What I'm figuring out now
Live thinking. Questions without answers yet. What you're reading, testing, noticing. Not expertise — curiosity in progress.
"The thing I'm still working out."
Theme 3
What I notice
Observations about design, AI, and work that most people don't say out loud. Small, specific, honest. No listicles — just one clear thought told well.
"The thing everyone's thinking but not saying."
Post format
Every post has the same shape
1
One specific moment or observation — not a general statement. A scene, a day, a conversation.
2
What it made you question — the honest tension. What you thought before. Why that changed.
3
Where you landed — or where you're still going — resolution or open question. Both are valid.
No listicles. No "here are 5 tips." No generic advice. Just one clear thought, told honestly.
